Teenage Mental Health Awareness

Jordan and the Middle East

by Ghazal AlK

The Growing Importance

Mental health among teenagers has become a major global issue. Adolescence is a critical developmental stage marked by emotional, social, and psychological changes. Studies show that approximately one in seven adolescents experiences mental health disorders, with anxiety and depression being the most common.

⚠️ Important: 1 in 7 teens globally faces mental health challenges — early awareness can save lives.

Mental Health in Jordan

Jordan has a very young population, making youth mental health especially important. Several factors increase risks:

  • Economic pressures and unemployment
  • Large refugee population
  • Educational and social stress
  • Exposure to regional conflict and instability
⚠️ Important: Nearly half of Jordan’s population is under 19 — adolescent mental health is crucial.

Cultural Barriers and Stigma

Stigma is one of the biggest barriers. Many people still associate mental illness with weakness or failure. Teenagers often feel isolated or afraid to speak openly about their struggles.

⚠️ Important: Stigma prevents teens from seeking help — open conversations save lives.

Solutions and Awareness

  • Encourage open discussions within families and schools
  • Educate students about emotional well-being and coping strategies
  • Improve access to professional mental health services
  • Launch public awareness campaigns to reduce stigma
⚠️ Important: Awareness + support systems = healthier, happier teens.

Support & Hotlines

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health, depression, or suicidal thoughts, reach out for help immediately.

  • Jordanian Mental Health Hotline: +962 79 888 8888
  • Embrace Help Center (Jordan): +962 65 555 555
  • Regional Online Support: iamaware.org

Remember: You are not alone. Thinking about reaching out is the first step.

⚠️ Important: Always reach out to a trusted adult, counselor, or hotline if you feel unsafe or distressed.
